Policies


1. Academic Integrity Policy:


(i). Defines academic misconduct (e.g., plagiarism, cheating, fabrication).

(ii). Outlines consequences for violations (e.g., failing grades, academic probation).

2. Equal Opportunity and Non-Discrimination Policy:


(i). Prohibits discrimination based on race, color, religion, gender, sexual orientation, disability, or other protected characteristics.

(ii). Establishes procedures for handling complaints and investigations.

Regulations


1. Academic Regulations:


(i). A student is considered in good standing if they are making normal progress toward a degree and have a satisfactory record in scholarship and conduct.

(ii). Undergraduates must include the sentence “This paper represents my own work in accordance with University regulations” at the end of their essays, laboratory reports, or other requirements.
